────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    PADA I — SAMADHI PADA (On Contemplation)
                           51 Sutras
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.1
Sanskrit:    अथ योगानुशासनम्
IAST:        atha yogānuśāsanam
English:     Now, the teachings of yoga begin.

1.2
Sanskrit:    योगश्चित्तवृत्तिनिरोधः
IAST:        yogaś citta-vṛtti-nirodhaḥ
English:     Yoga is the cessation of the fluctuations of the mind.

1.3
Sanskrit:    तदा द्रष्टुः स्वरूपेऽवस्थानम्
IAST:        tadā draṣṭuḥ svarūpe 'vasthānam
English:     Then the Seer abides in its own true nature.

1.4
Sanskrit:    वृत्तिसारूप्यमितरत्र
IAST:        vṛtti-sārūpyam itaratra
English:     Otherwise, the Seer identifies with the fluctuations of the mind.

1.5
Sanskrit:    वृत्तयः पञ्चतय्यः क्लिष्टाक्लिष्टाः
IAST:        vṛttayaḥ pañcatayyaḥ kliṣṭākliṣṭāḥ
English:     There are five kinds of mental fluctuations, which are either afflicted or non-afflicted.

1.6
Sanskrit:    प्रमाणविपर्ययविकल्पनिद्रास्मृतयः
IAST:        pramāṇa-viparyaya-vikalpa-nidrā-smṛtayaḥ
English:     They are: right knowledge, error, imagination, deep sleep, and memory.

1.7
Sanskrit:    प्रत्यक्षानुमानागमाः प्रमाणानि
IAST:        pratyakṣānumānāgamāḥ pramāṇāni
English:     Right knowledge arises from direct perception, inference, and reliable testimony.

1.8
Sanskrit:    विपर्ययो मिथ्याज्ञानमतद्रूपप्रतिष्ठम्
IAST:        viparyayo mithyā-jñānam atad-rūpa-pratiṣṭham
English:     Error is false knowledge not based on the true form of the object.

1.9
Sanskrit:    शब्दज्ञानानुपाती वस्तुशून्यो विकल्पः
IAST:        śabda-jñānānupātī vastu-śūnyo vikalpaḥ
English:     Imagination is knowledge based on words that has no corresponding object in reality.

1.10
Sanskrit:    अभावप्रत्ययालम्बना वृत्तिर्निद्रा
IAST:        abhāva-pratyayālambanā vṛttir nidrā
English:     Deep sleep is the mental fluctuation that rests on the experience of nothingness.

1.11
Sanskrit:    अनुभूतविषयासम्प्रमोषः स्मृतिः
IAST:        anubhūta-viṣayāsampramoṣaḥ smṛtiḥ
English:     Memory is the retention of experienced objects without forgetting them.

1.12
Sanskrit:    अभ्यासवैराग्याभ्यां तन्निरोधः
IAST:        abhyāsa-vairāgyābhyāṃ tan-nirodhaḥ
English:     These fluctuations are restrained by practice and non-attachment.

1.13
Sanskrit:    तत्र स्थितौ यत्नोऽभ्यासः
IAST:        tatra sthitau yatno 'bhyāsaḥ
English:     Of these two, practice is the effort to remain in a steady, stable state.

1.14
Sanskrit:    स तु दीर्घकालनैरन्तर्यसत्काराऽऽसेवितो दृढभूमिः
IAST:        sa tu dīrgha-kāla-nairantarya-satkārāsevito dṛḍha-bhūmiḥ
English:     This practice becomes firmly established when pursued continuously over a long time, with sincerity and earnestness.

1.15
Sanskrit:    दृष्टानुश्रविकविषयवितृष्णस्य वशीकारसंज्ञा वैराग्यम्
IAST:        dṛṣṭānuśravika-viṣaya-vitṛṣṇasya vaśīkāra-saṃjñā vairāgyam
English:     Non-attachment is the mastery of consciousness — a freedom from craving for objects seen or described in scripture.

1.16
Sanskrit:    तत्परं पुरुषख्यातेर्गुणवैतृष्ण्यम्
IAST:        tat paraṃ puruṣa-khyāter guṇa-vaitṛṣṇyam
English:     Supreme non-attachment is freedom from the very qualities of nature, arising from the direct vision of the Self.

1.17
Sanskrit:    वितर्कविचारानन्दास्मितारूपानुगमात्सम्प्रज्ञातः
IAST:        vitarka-vicārānandāsmitā-rūpānugamāt samprajñātaḥ
English:     Cognitive samadhi is accompanied by deliberation, reflection, bliss, and a sense of pure being.

1.18
Sanskrit:    विरामप्रत्ययाभ्यासपूर्वः संस्कारशेषोऽन्यः
IAST:        virāma-pratyayābhyāsa-pūrvaḥ saṃskāra-śeṣo 'nyaḥ
English:     The other samadhi — preceded by the practice of cessation — leaves only latent impressions behind.

1.19
Sanskrit:    भवप्रत्ययो विदेहप्रकृतिलयानाम्
IAST:        bhava-pratyayo videha-prakṛtilayānām
English:     For the disembodied and those merged into Nature, the state of samadhi arises from their very existence.

1.20
Sanskrit:    श्रद्धावीर्यस्मृतिसमाधिप्रज्ञापूर्वक इतरेषाम्
IAST:        śraddhā-vīrya-smṛti-samādhi-prajñā-pūrvaka itareṣām
English:     For others, it is preceded by faith, energy, memory, absorption, and discernment.

1.21
Sanskrit:    तीव्रसंवेगानामासन्नः
IAST:        tīvra-saṃvegānām āsannaḥ
English:     For those with intense ardor, samadhi is near.

1.22
Sanskrit:    मृदुमध्याधिमात्रत्वात्ततोऽपि विशेषः
IAST:        mṛdu-madhyādhimātratvāt tato 'pi viśeṣaḥ
English:     Even among those, there are distinctions of mild, moderate, and intense practice.

1.23
Sanskrit:    ईश्वरप्रणिधानाद्वा
IAST:        īśvara-praṇidhānād vā
English:     Or, samadhi is attained through devotion to Ishvara (God).

1.24
Sanskrit:    क्लेशकर्मविपाकाशयैरपरामृष्टः पुरुषविशेष ईश्वरः
IAST:        kleśa-karma-vipākāśayair aparāmṛṣṭaḥ puruṣa-viśeṣa īśvaraḥ
English:     Ishvara is a special Purusha, untouched by afflictions, karma, the fruits of karma, and subliminal impressions.

1.25
Sanskrit:    तत्र निरतिशयं सर्वज्ञबीजम्
IAST:        tatra niratiśayaṃ sarva-jña-bījam
English:     In Ishvara is the unsurpassed seed of omniscience.

1.26
Sanskrit:    स एष पूर्वेषामपि गुरुः कालेनानवच्छेदात्
IAST:        sa eṣa pūrveṣām api guruḥ kālenānavacchedāt
English:     Being unconditioned by time, Ishvara is the teacher of even the ancient teachers.

1.27
Sanskrit:    तस्य वाचकः प्रणवः
IAST:        tasya vācakaḥ praṇavaḥ
English:     His name is the sacred syllable OM (Pranava).

1.28
Sanskrit:    तज्जपस्तदर्थभावनम्
IAST:        taj-japas tad-artha-bhāvanam
English:     The repetition of OM should be done with contemplation of its meaning.

1.29
Sanskrit:    ततः प्रत्यक्चेतनाधिगमोऽप्यन्तरायाभावश्च
IAST:        tataḥ pratyak-cetanādhigamo 'py antarāyābhāvaś ca
English:     From this practice comes the realization of inner consciousness and the disappearance of obstacles.

1.30
Sanskrit:    व्याधिस्त्यानसंशयप्रमादालस्याविरतिभ्रान्तिदर्शनालब्धभूमिकत्वानवस्थितत्वानि चित्तविक्षेपास्तेऽन्तरायाः
IAST:        vyādhi-styāna-saṃśaya-pramādālasyāvirati-bhrānti-darśanālabdha-bhūmikatvānavasthitatvāni citta-vikṣepās te 'ntarāyāḥ
English:     The obstacles that distract the mind are: disease, dullness, doubt, heedlessness, sloth, intemperance, false perception, failure to reach a stage, and instability in a stage.

1.31
Sanskrit:    दुःखदौर्मनस्याङ्गमेजयत्वश्वासप्रश्वासा विक्षेपसहभुवः
IAST:        duḥkha-daurmanasyāṅgam-ejayatva-śvāsa-praśvāsā vikṣepa-sahabhuvaḥ
English:     Pain, despair, trembling of the body, and irregular breathing accompany the distractions.

1.32
Sanskrit:    तत्प्रतिषेधार्थमेकतत्त्वाभ्यासः
IAST:        tat-pratiṣedhārtham eka-tattvābhyāsaḥ
English:     To remove these obstacles, practice on a single truth.

1.33
Sanskrit:    मैत्रीकरुणामुदितोपेक्षाणां सुखदुःखपुण्यापुण्यविषयाणां भावनातश्चित्तप्रसादनम्
IAST:        maitrī-karuṇā-muditopekṣāṇāṃ sukha-duḥkha-puṇyāpuṇya-viṣayāṇāṃ bhāvanātaś citta-prasādanam
English:     The mind becomes serene by cultivating friendliness toward the happy, compassion for the suffering, joy for the virtuous, and equanimity toward the wicked.

1.34
Sanskrit:    प्रच्छर्दनविधारणाभ्यां वा प्राणस्य
IAST:        pracchardana-vidhāraṇābhyāṃ vā prāṇasya
English:     Or by the exhalation and retention of breath.

1.35
Sanskrit:    विषयवती वा प्रवृत्तिरुत्पन्ना मनसः स्थितिनिबन्धनी
IAST:        viṣayavatī vā pravṛttir utpannā manasaḥ sthiti-nibandhanī
English:     Or, a higher sense perception arising can steady the mind.

1.36
Sanskrit:    विशोका वा ज्योतिष्मती
IAST:        viśokā vā jyotiṣmatī
English:     Or by focusing on the sorrowless effulgent light within.

1.37
Sanskrit:    वीतरागविषयं वा चित्तम्
IAST:        vīta-rāga-viṣayaṃ vā cittam
English:     Or by meditating on the mind of one who is free from passion.

1.38
Sanskrit:    स्वप्ननिद्राज्ञानालम्बनं वा
IAST:        svapna-nidrā-jñānālambanam vā
English:     Or by resting on knowledge coming from dreams or deep sleep.

1.39
Sanskrit:    यथाभिमतध्यानाद्वा
IAST:        yathābhimata-dhyānād vā
English:     Or by meditating on whatever is pleasing to one.

1.40
Sanskrit:    परमाणुपरममहत्त्वान्तोऽस्य वशीकारः
IAST:        paramāṇu-parama-mahattānto 'sya vaśīkāraḥ
English:     Mastery extends from the smallest atom to the greatest infinity.

1.41
Sanskrit:    क्षीणवृत्तेरभिजातस्येव मणेर्ग्रहीतृग्रहणग्राह्येषु तत्स्थतदञ्जनता समापत्तिः
IAST:        kṣīṇa-vṛtter abhijātasyeva maṇer grahītṛ-grahaṇa-grāhyeṣu tat-stha-tad-añjanatā samāpattiḥ
English:     When the fluctuations are diminished, the mind becomes like a transparent jewel — taking on the color of whatever is near, be it the knower, the act of knowing, or the known. This is absorption (samapatti).

1.42
Sanskrit:    तत्र शब्दार्थज्ञानविकल्पैः संकीर्णा सवितर्का समापत्तिः
IAST:        tatra śabdārtha-jñāna-vikalpaiḥ saṃkīrṇā savitarkā samāpattiḥ
English:     Absorption with deliberation (savitarka) is mixed with word, meaning, and conceptual knowledge.

1.43
Sanskrit:    स्मृतिपरिशुद्धौ स्वरूपशून्येवार्थमात्रनिर्भासा निर्वितर्का
IAST:        smṛti-pariśuddhau svarūpa-śūnyevārtha-mātra-nirbhāsā nirvitarkā
English:     When memory is purified and the mind shines as the object alone — empty of its own nature — that is absorption without deliberation (nirvitarka).

1.44
Sanskrit:    एतयैव सविचारा निर्विचारा च सूक्ष्मविषया व्याख्याता
IAST:        etayaiva savicārā nirvicārā ca sūkṣma-viṣayā vyākhyātā
English:     In the same way, absorptions with and without reflection on subtle objects are explained.

1.45
Sanskrit:    सूक्ष्मविषयत्वं चालिङ्गपर्यवसानम्
IAST:        sūkṣma-viṣayatvaṃ cāliṅga-paryavasānam
English:     The subtlety of objects culminates in the undifferentiated (alinga — pure primordial nature).

1.46
Sanskrit:    ता एव सबीजः समाधिः
IAST:        tā eva sabījaḥ samādhiḥ
English:     These absorptions are samadhi with seed.

1.47
Sanskrit:    निर्विचारवैशारद्येऽध्यात्मप्रसादः
IAST:        nirvicāra-vaiśāradye 'dhyātma-prasādaḥ
English:     In the clarity of nirvicara samadhi, the light of the inner Self dawns.

1.48
Sanskrit:    ऋतम्भरा तत्र प्रज्ञा
IAST:        ṛtambharā tatra prajñā
English:     The wisdom born there is truth-bearing (ritambhara).

1.49
Sanskrit:    श्रुतानुमानप्रज्ञाभ्यामन्यविषया विशेषार्थत्वात्
IAST:        śrutānumāna-prajñābhyām anya-viṣayā viśeṣārthatvāt
English:     This wisdom has a different object than knowledge from scripture or inference, because it pertains to the particular.

1.50
Sanskrit:    तज्जः संस्कारोऽन्यसंस्कारप्रतिबन्धी
IAST:        taj-jaḥ saṃskāro 'nya-saṃskāra-pratibandhī
English:     The impression born of this wisdom obstructs all other impressions.

1.51
Sanskrit:    तस्यापि निरोधे सर्वनिरोधान्निर्बीजः समाधिः
IAST:        tasyāpi nirodhe sarva-nirodhān nirbījaḥ samādhiḥ
English:     When even this impression is restrained — all being restrained — seedless samadhi arises.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    PADA II — SADHANA PADA (On Practice)
                           55 Sutras
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.1
Sanskrit:    तपःस्वाध्यायेश्वरप्रणिधानानि क्रियायोगः
IAST:        tapaḥ-svādhyāyeśvara-praṇidhānāni kriyā-yogaḥ
English:     Kriya yoga consists of: austerity (tapas), self-study (svadhyaya), and devotion to Ishvara.

2.2
Sanskrit:    समाधिभावनार्थः क्लेशतनूकरणार्थश्च
IAST:        samādhi-bhāvanārthaḥ kleśa-tanūkaraṇārthaś ca
English:     Its purpose is to cultivate samadhi and to attenuate the afflictions.

2.3
Sanskrit:    अविद्यास्मितारागद्वेषाभिनिवेशाः क्लेशाः
IAST:        avidyāsmitā-rāga-dveṣābhiniveśāḥ kleśāḥ
English:     The afflictions are: ignorance, ego, attachment, aversion, and fear of death.

2.4
Sanskrit:    अविद्या क्षेत्रमुत्तरेषां प्रसुप्ततनुविच्छिन्नोदाराणाम्
IAST:        avidyā kṣetram uttareṣāṃ prasupta-tanu-vicchinnodārāṇām
English:     Ignorance is the field for the other afflictions, whether dormant, weakened, alternating, or active.

2.5
Sanskrit:    अनित्याशुचिदुःखानात्मसु नित्यशुचिसुखात्मख्यातिरविद्या
IAST:        anitya-aśuci-duḥkhānātmasu nitya-śuci-sukhātma-khyātir avidyā
English:     Ignorance is taking the impermanent, impure, painful, and non-Self to be the permanent, pure, pleasurable, and Self.

2.6
Sanskrit:    दृग्दर्शनशक्त्योरेकात्मतेवास्मिता
IAST:        dṛg-darśana-śaktyor ekātmatevāsmitā
English:     Ego (asmita) is the identification of the Seer with the power of seeing.

2.7
Sanskrit:    सुखानुशयी रागः
IAST:        sukhānuśayī rāgaḥ
English:     Attachment is that which dwells upon pleasure.

2.8
Sanskrit:    दुःखानुशयी द्वेषः
IAST:        duḥkhānuśayī dveṣaḥ
English:     Aversion is that which dwells upon pain.

2.9
Sanskrit:    स्वरसवाही विदुषोऽपि तथारूढोऽभिनिवेशः
IAST:        svarasavāhī viduṣo 'pi tathā rūḍho 'bhiniveśaḥ
English:     Fear of death flows by its own momentum — it exists even in the wise.

2.10
Sanskrit:    ते प्रतिप्रसवहेयाः सूक्ष्माः
IAST:        te pratiprasava-heyāḥ sūkṣmāḥ
English:     In their subtle form, these afflictions are overcome by resolving them back into their origin.

2.11
Sanskrit:    ध्यानहेयास्तद्वृत्तयः
IAST:        dhyāna-heyās tad-vṛttayaḥ
English:     Their active fluctuations are overcome through meditation.

2.12
Sanskrit:    क्लेशमूलः कर्माशयो दृष्टादृष्टजन्मवेदनीयः
IAST:        kleśa-mūlaḥ karmāśayo dṛṣṭādṛṣṭa-janma-vedanīyaḥ
English:     The storehouse of karma is rooted in the afflictions and ripens in seen and unseen births.

2.13
Sanskrit:    सति मूले तद्विपाको जात्यायुर्भोगाः
IAST:        sati mūle tad-vipāko jāty-āyur-bhogāḥ
English:     As long as the root exists, karma ripens into birth, lifespan, and experience.

2.14
Sanskrit:    ते ह्लादपरितापफलाः पुण्यापुण्यहेतुत्वात्
IAST:        te hlāda-paritāpa-phalāḥ puṇyāpuṇya-hetutvāt
English:     They bear fruit of joy or sorrow according to whether their cause is virtue or vice.

2.15
Sanskrit:    परिणामतापसंस्कारदुःखैर्गुणवृत्तिविरोधाच्च दुःखमेव सर्वं विवेकिनः
IAST:        pariṇāma-tāpa-saṃskāra-duḥkhair guṇa-vṛtti-virodhāc ca duḥkham eva sarvaṃ vivekinaḥ
English:     To the discerning one, everything is suffering — due to the pain of change, the pain of anxiety, and the pain of impressions, all in conflict with the ever-changing qualities of nature.

2.16
Sanskrit:    हेयं दुःखमनागतम्
IAST:        heyaṃ duḥkham anāgatam
English:     The suffering that has not yet come is to be avoided.

2.17
Sanskrit:    द्रष्टृदृश्ययोः संयोगो हेयहेतुः
IAST:        draṣṭṛ-dṛśyayoḥ saṃyogo heya-hetuḥ
English:     The cause of that suffering to be avoided is the union of the Seer and the seen.

2.18
Sanskrit:    प्रकाशक्रियास्थितिशीलं भूतेन्द्रियात्मकं भोगापवर्गार्थं दृश्यम्
IAST:        prakāśa-kriyā-sthiti-śīlaṃ bhūtendriyātmakaṃ bhogāpavargārthaṃ dṛśyam
English:     The seen — consisting of the elements and senses — has the nature of luminosity, activity, and inertia. Its purpose is experience and liberation.

2.19
Sanskrit:    विशेषाविशेषलिङ्गमात्रालिङ्गानि गुणपर्वाणि
IAST:        viśeṣa-aviśeṣa-liṅgamātra-aliṅgāni guṇa-parvāṇi
English:     The stages of the qualities (gunas) are: the differentiated, the undifferentiated, the indicated, and the unmanifest.

2.20
Sanskrit:    द्रष्टा दृशिमात्रः शुद्धोऽपि प्रत्ययानुपश्यः
IAST:        draṣṭā dṛśimātraḥ śuddho 'pi pratyayānupaśyaḥ
English:     The Seer is pure consciousness alone; although pure, it appears to see through the mind's ideas.

2.21
Sanskrit:    तदर्थ एव दृश्यस्यात्मा
IAST:        tad-artha eva dṛśyasyātmā
English:     The very nature of the seen exists for the sake of the Seer alone.

2.22
Sanskrit:    कृतार्थं प्रति नष्टमप्यनष्टं तदन्यसाधारणत्वात्
IAST:        kṛtārthaṃ prati naṣṭam apy anaṣṭaṃ tad-anya-sādhāraṇatvāt
English:     Though the seen ceases to exist for one who has achieved the purpose, it continues for others, as it is common to all.

2.23
Sanskrit:    स्वस्वामिशक्त्योः स्वरूपोपलब्धिहेतुः संयोगः
IAST:        sva-svāmi-śaktyoḥ svarūpopalabdhi-hetuḥ saṃyogaḥ
English:     The union of the owner (Seer) and the owned (seen) is for the purpose of recognizing the true nature of both.

2.24
Sanskrit:    तस्य हेतुरविद्या
IAST:        tasya hetur avidyā
English:     The cause of this union is ignorance.

2.25
Sanskrit:    तदभावात्संयोगाभावो हानं तद्दृशेः कैवल्यम्
IAST:        tad-abhāvāt saṃyogābhāvo hānaṃ tad-dṛśeḥ kaivalyam
English:     With the removal of ignorance, the union ceases — that is liberation, the aloneness of the Seer.

2.26
Sanskrit:    विवेकख्यातिरविप्लवा हानोपायः
IAST:        viveka-khyātir aviplavā hānopāyaḥ
English:     The uninterrupted practice of discriminative discernment is the means to liberation.

2.27
Sanskrit:    तस्य सप्तधा प्रान्तभूमिः प्रज्ञा
IAST:        tasya saptadhā prānta-bhūmiḥ prajñā
English:     The wisdom of one on the final stage unfolds in seven stages.

2.28
Sanskrit:    योगाङ्गानुष्ठानादशुद्धिक्षये ज्ञानदीप्तिराविवेकख्यातेः
IAST:        yogāṅgānuṣṭhānād aśuddhi-kṣaye jñāna-dīptir ā-viveka-khyāteḥ
English:     Through the practice of the limbs of yoga, as impurity diminishes, the light of knowledge shines until discriminative discernment is reached.

2.29
Sanskrit:    यमनियमासनप्राणायामप्रत्याहारधारणाध्यानसमाधयोऽष्टावङ्गानि
IAST:        yama-niyamāsana-prāṇāyāma-pratyāhāra-dhāraṇā-dhyāna-samādhayo 'ṣṭāv aṅgāni
English:     The eight limbs of yoga are: yama, niyama, asana, pranayama, pratyahara, dharana, dhyana, and samadhi.

2.30
Sanskrit:    अहिंसासत्यास्तेयब्रह्मचर्यापरिग्रहा यमाः
IAST:        ahiṃsā-satyāsteya-brahmacarya-aparigrahā yamāḥ
English:     The yamas are: non-violence, truthfulness, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possessiveness.

2.31
Sanskrit:    जातिदेशकालसमयानवच्छिन्नाः सार्वभौमा महाव्रतम्
IAST:        jāti-deśa-kāla-samayānavacchinnāḥ sārvabhaumā mahā-vratam
English:     These, when universal and not limited by class, place, time, or circumstance, constitute the great vow.

2.32
Sanskrit:    शौचसन्तोषतपःस्वाध्यायेश्वरप्रणिधानानि नियमाः
IAST:        śauca-santoṣa-tapaḥ-svādhyāyeśvara-praṇidhānāni niyamāḥ
English:     The niyamas are: purity, contentment, austerity, self-study, and devotion to Ishvara.

2.33
Sanskrit:    वितर्कबाधने प्रतिपक्षभावनम्
IAST:        vitarka-bādhane pratipakṣa-bhāvanam
English:     When disturbed by negative thoughts, cultivate the opposite.

2.34
Sanskrit:    वितर्का हिंसादयः कृतकारितानुमोदिता लोभक्रोधमोहपूर्वका मृदुमध्याधिमात्रा दुःखाज्ञानानन्तफला इति प्रतिपक्षभावनम्
IAST:        vitarkā hiṃsādayaḥ kṛta-kāritānumoditā lobha-krodha-moha-pūrvakā mṛdu-madhyādhimātrā duḥkhājñānānanta-phalā iti pratipakṣa-bhāvanam
English:     Negative thoughts — such as violence, whether done, caused, or condoned, arising from greed, anger, or delusion, whether mild, moderate, or intense — result in endless suffering and ignorance; thus, cultivate the opposite.

2.35
Sanskrit:    अहिंसाप्रतिष्ठायां तत्सन्निधौ वैरत्यागः
IAST:        ahiṃsā-pratiṣṭhāyāṃ tat-sannidhau vaira-tyāgaḥ
English:     When one is established in non-violence, all hostility ceases in one's presence.

2.36
Sanskrit:    सत्यप्रतिष्ठायां क्रियाफलाश्रयत्वम्
IAST:        satya-pratiṣṭhāyāṃ kriyā-phalāśrayatvam
English:     When established in truthfulness, actions and their fruits become dependent on one.

2.37
Sanskrit:    अस्तेयप्रतिष्ठायां सर्वरत्नोपस्थानम्
IAST:        asteya-pratiṣṭhāyāṃ sarva-ratnopasthānam
English:     When established in non-stealing, all jewels present themselves.

2.38
Sanskrit:    ब्रह्मचर्यप्रतिष्ठायां वीर्यलाभः
IAST:        brahmacarya-pratiṣṭhāyāṃ vīrya-lābhaḥ
English:     When established in celibacy, great energy (virya) is gained.

2.39
Sanskrit:    अपरिग्रहस्थैर्ये जन्मकथंतासम्बोधः
IAST:        aparigraha-sthairye janma-kathaṃtā-sambodhaḥ
English:     When established in non-possessiveness, knowledge of the how and why of birth arises.

2.40
Sanskrit:    शौचात्स्वाङ्गजुगुप्सा परैरसंसर्गः
IAST:        śaucāt svāṅga-jugupsā parair asaṃsargaḥ
English:     From purity arises disgust for one's own body and disinclinaton for contact with others.

2.41
Sanskrit:    सत्त्वशुद्धिसौमनस्यैकाग्र्येन्द्रियजयात्मदर्शनयोग्यत्वानि च
IAST:        sattva-śuddhi-saumanasyaikāgryendriya-jayātma-darśana-yogyatvāni ca
English:     Also: purity of mind, cheerfulness, one-pointedness, mastery of the senses, and fitness for the vision of the Self.

2.42
Sanskrit:    सन्तोषादनुत्तमः सुखलाभः
IAST:        santoṣād anuttamaḥ sukha-lābhaḥ
English:     From contentment comes supreme happiness.

2.43
Sanskrit:    कायेन्द्रियसिद्धिरशुद्धिक्षयात्तपसः
IAST:        kāyendriya-siddhir aśuddhi-kṣayāt tapasaḥ
English:     Through austerity and the destruction of impurity, perfection of body and senses arises.

2.44
Sanskrit:    स्वाध्यायादिष्टदेवतासम्प्रयोगः
IAST:        svādhyāyād iṣṭa-devatā-samprayogaḥ
English:     From self-study comes union with the desired deity.

2.45
Sanskrit:    समाधिसिद्धिरीश्वरप्रणिधानात्
IAST:        samādhi-siddhir īśvara-praṇidhānāt
English:     Perfection in samadhi comes from devotion to Ishvara.

2.46
Sanskrit:    स्थिरसुखमासनम्
IAST:        sthira-sukham āsanam
English:     Asana is a steady and comfortable posture.

2.47
Sanskrit:    प्रयत्नशैथिल्यानन्तसमापत्तिभ्याम्
IAST:        prayatna-śaithilyānanta-samāpattibhyām
English:     It is perfected by relaxation of effort and absorption in the infinite.

2.48
Sanskrit:    ततो द्वन्द्वानभिघातः
IAST:        tato dvandvānabhighātaḥ
English:     From that, one is undisturbed by the pairs of opposites.

2.49
Sanskrit:    तस्मिन्सति श्वासप्रश्वासयोर्गतिविच्छेदः प्राणायामः
IAST:        tasmin sati śvāsa-praśvāsayor gati-vicchedaḥ prāṇāyāmaḥ
English:     When that is accomplished, pranayama is the regulation of the flow of inhalation and exhalation.

2.50
Sanskrit:    बाह्याभ्यन्तरस्तम्भवृत्तिर्देशकालसङ्ख्याभिः परिदृष्टो दीर्घसूक्ष्मः
IAST:        bāhyābhyantara-stambha-vṛttir deśa-kāla-saṃkhyābhiḥ paridṛṣṭo dīrgha-sūkṣmaḥ
English:     Pranayama has external, internal, and restrained movements, regulated by place, time, and number — becoming prolonged and subtle.

2.51
Sanskrit:    बाह्याभ्यन्तरविषयाक्षेपी चतुर्थः
IAST:        bāhyābhyantara-viṣayākṣepī caturthaḥ
English:     The fourth type transcends the external and internal.

2.52
Sanskrit:    ततः क्षीयते प्रकाशावरणम्
IAST:        tataḥ kṣīyate prakāśāvaraṇam
English:     As a result, the veil over the inner light dissolves.

2.53
Sanskrit:    धारणासु च योग्यता मनसः
IAST:        dhāraṇāsu ca yogyatā manasaḥ
English:     And the mind becomes fit for concentration (dharana).

2.54
Sanskrit:    स्वविषयासम्प्रयोगे चित्तस्वरूपानुकार इवेन्द्रियाणां प्रत्याहारः
IAST:        sva-viṣayāsamprayoge citta-svarūpānukāra ivendriyāṇāṃ pratyāhāraḥ
English:     When the senses withdraw from their objects and imitate the nature of the mind, that is pratyahara.

2.55
Sanskrit:    ततः परमा वश्यतेन्द्रियाणाम्
IAST:        tataḥ paramā vaśyatendriyāṇām
English:     From that comes supreme mastery of the senses.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    PADA III — VIBHUTI PADA (On Powers)
                           56 Sutras
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.1
Sanskrit:    देशबन्धश्चित्तस्य धारणा
IAST:        deśa-bandhaś cittasya dhāraṇā
English:     Dharana is the binding of the mind to a place (object of focus).

3.2
Sanskrit:    तत्र प्रत्ययैकतानता ध्यानम्
IAST:        tatra pratyayaikatānatā dhyānam
English:     Dhyana is the continuous flow of cognition toward that object.

3.3
Sanskrit:    तदेवार्थमात्रनिर्भासं स्वरूपशून्यमिव समाधिः
IAST:        tad evārtha-mātra-nirbhāsaṃ svarūpa-śūnyam iva samādhiḥ
English:     When that same meditation shines forth as the object alone — empty of its own form — that is samadhi.

3.4
Sanskrit:    त्रयमेकत्र संयमः
IAST:        trayam ekatra saṃyamaḥ
English:     The three together (dharana, dhyana, samadhi) practiced on one object is samyama.

3.5
Sanskrit:    तज्जयात्प्रज्ञालोकः
IAST:        taj-jayāt prajñālokaḥ
English:     From mastery of samyama comes the light of insight (prajna).

3.6
Sanskrit:    तस्य भूमिषु विनियोगः
IAST:        tasya bhūmiṣu viniyogaḥ
English:     Its application proceeds stage by stage.

3.7
Sanskrit:    त्रयमन्तरङ्गं पूर्वेभ्यः
IAST:        trayam antaraṅgaṃ pūrvebhyaḥ
English:     These three (dharana, dhyana, samadhi) are more inner than the preceding five limbs.

3.8
Sanskrit:    तदपि बहिरङ्गं निर्बीजस्य
IAST:        tad api bahiraṅgaṃ nirbījasya
English:     But even these three are external to seedless samadhi.

3.9
Sanskrit:    व्युत्थाननिरोधसंस्कारयोरभिभवप्रादुर्भावौ निरोधक्षणचित्तान्वयो निरोधपरिणामः
IAST:        vyutthāna-nirodha-saṃskārayor abhibhava-prādurbhāvau nirodha-kṣaṇa-cittānvayo nirodha-pariṇāmaḥ
English:     The transformation toward restraint is the mind's conjunction with the moment of restraint, as the impressions of emergence are suppressed and those of restraint appear.

3.10
Sanskrit:    तस्य प्रशान्तवाहिता संस्कारात्
IAST:        tasya praśānta-vāhitā saṃskārāt
English:     The steady flow of this restraint comes through impressions.

3.11
Sanskrit:    सर्वार्थतैकाग्रतयोः क्षयोदयौ चित्तस्य समाधिपरिणामः
IAST:        sarvārthataikāgratayoḥ kṣayodayau cittasya samādhi-pariṇāmaḥ
English:     The transformation into samadhi is the decline of all-objectness and the rise of one-pointedness in the mind.

3.12
Sanskrit:    ततः पुनः शान्तोदितौ तुल्यप्रत्ययौ चित्तस्यैकाग्रतापरिणामः
IAST:        tataḥ punaḥ śāntoditau tulya-pratyayau cittasyaikāgratā-pariṇāmaḥ
English:     Then again, the transformation to one-pointedness is when the subsiding and arising cognitions are identical.

3.13
Sanskrit:    एतेन भूतेन्द्रियेषु धर्मलक्षणावस्थापरिणामा व्याख्याताः
IAST:        etena bhūtendriyeṣu dharma-lakṣaṇāvasthā-pariṇāmā vyākhyātāḥ
English:     By this, the transformations of characteristic, temporal mark, and condition in the elements and senses are explained.

3.14
Sanskrit:    शान्तोदिताव्यपदेश्यधर्मानुपाती धर्मी
IAST:        śāntoditāvyapadeśya-dharmānupātī dharmī
English:     The substratum is that which has past, present, and indescribable characteristics in succession.

3.15
Sanskrit:    क्रमान्यत्वं परिणामान्यत्वे हेतुः
IAST:        kramānyatvaṃ pariṇāmānyatve hetuḥ
English:     The cause of the difference in transformation is the difference in the sequence.

3.16
Sanskrit:    परिणामत्रयसंयमादतीतानागतज्ञानम्
IAST:        pariṇāma-traya-saṃyamād atītānāgata-jñānam
English:     From samyama on the three transformations comes knowledge of past and future.

3.17
Sanskrit:    शब्दार्थप्रत्ययानामितरेतराध्यासात्संकरस्तत्प्रविभागसंयमात्सर्वभूतरुतज्ञानम्
IAST:        śabdārtha-pratyayānām itaretarādhyāsāt saṃkaras tat-pravibhāga-saṃyamāt sarva-bhūta-ruta-jñānam
English:     Word, meaning, and cognition are ordinarily confused by superimposition; from samyama on their distinction comes knowledge of the sounds of all beings.

3.18
Sanskrit:    संस्कारसाक्षात्करणात्पूर्वजातिज्ञानम्
IAST:        saṃskāra-sākṣātkaraṇāt pūrva-jāti-jñānam
English:     From direct perception of impressions comes knowledge of previous births.

3.19
Sanskrit:    प्रत्ययस्य परचित्तज्ञानम्
IAST:        pratyayasya para-citta-jñānam
English:     From samyama on another's cognition comes knowledge of that other mind.

3.20
Sanskrit:    न च तत्सालम्बनं तस्याविषयीभूतत्वात्
IAST:        na ca tat sālambanaṃ tasyāviṣayī-bhūtatvāt
English:     But not the support of that cognition, since that is not the object of the samyama.

3.21
Sanskrit:    कायरूपसंयमात्तद्ग्राह्यशक्तिस्तम्भे चक्षुःप्रकाशासम्प्रयोगेऽन्तर्धानम्
IAST:        kāya-rūpa-saṃyamāt tad-grāhya-śakti-stambhe cakṣuḥ-prakāśāsamprayoge 'ntardhānam
English:     From samyama on the form of the body, and by suspending the power of visibility, disappearance occurs — the light to the eye being cut off.

3.22
Sanskrit:    सोपक्रमं निरुपक्रमं च कर्म तत्संयमादपरान्तज्ञानमरिष्टेभ्यो वा
IAST:        sopakramaṃ nirupakramaṃ ca karma tat-saṃyamād aparānta-jñānam ariṣṭebhyo vā
English:     Karma is either fast-ripening or slow-ripening; from samyama on this, or from omens, comes foreknowledge of death.

3.23
Sanskrit:    मैत्र्यादिषु बलानि
IAST:        maitryādiṣu balāni
English:     From samyama on friendliness and other virtues, their respective powers arise.

3.24
Sanskrit:    बलेषु हस्तिबलादीनि
IAST:        baleṣu hasti-balādīni
English:     From samyama on strengths, the strength of an elephant and so forth.

3.25
Sanskrit:    प्रवृत्त्यालोकन्यासात्सूक्ष्मव्यवहितविप्रकृष्टज्ञानम्
IAST:        pravṛtty-āloka-nyāsāt sūkṣma-vyavahita-viprakṛṣṭa-jñānam
English:     By directing the light of higher perception, knowledge of the subtle, hidden, and distant arises.

3.26
Sanskrit:    भुवनज्ञानं सूर्ये संयमात्
IAST:        bhuvana-jñānaṃ sūrye saṃyamāt
English:     From samyama on the sun comes knowledge of the worlds.

3.27
Sanskrit:    चन्द्रे ताराव्यूहज्ञानम्
IAST:        candre tārā-vyūha-jñānam
English:     From samyama on the moon comes knowledge of the arrangement of the stars.

3.28
Sanskrit:    ध्रुवे तद्गतिज्ञानम्
IAST:        dhruve tad-gati-jñānam
English:     From samyama on the North Star comes knowledge of its movement.

3.29
Sanskrit:    नाभिचक्रे कायव्यूहज्ञानम्
IAST:        nābhi-cakre kāya-vyūha-jñānam
English:     From samyama on the navel chakra comes knowledge of the organization of the body.

3.30
Sanskrit:    कण्ठकूपे क्षुत्पिपासानिवृत्तिः
IAST:        kaṇṭha-kūpe kṣut-pipāsā-nivṛttiḥ
English:     From samyama on the throat pit comes cessation of hunger and thirst.

3.31
Sanskrit:    कूर्मनाड्यां स्थैर्यम्
IAST:        kūrma-nāḍyāṃ sthairyam
English:     From samyama on the kurma nadi comes steadiness.

3.32
Sanskrit:    मूर्धज्योतिषि सिद्धदर्शनम्
IAST:        mūrdha-jyotiṣi siddha-darśanam
English:     From samyama on the light in the head comes the vision of perfected beings.

3.33
Sanskrit:    प्रातिभाद्वा सर्वम्
IAST:        prātibhād vā sarvam
English:     Or, from spontaneous illumination (pratibha), all knowledge.

3.34
Sanskrit:    हृदये चित्तसंवित्
IAST:        hṛdaye citta-saṃvit
English:     From samyama on the heart comes knowledge of the mind.

3.35
Sanskrit:    सत्त्वपुरुषयोरत्यन्तासंकीर्णयोः प्रत्ययाविशेषो भोगः परार्थत्वात्स्वार्थसंयमात्पुरुषज्ञानम्
IAST:        sattva-puruṣayor atyantāsaṃkīrṇayoḥ pratyayāviśeṣo bhogaḥ parārthatvāt svārtha-saṃyamāt puruṣa-jñānam
English:     Experience arises from the non-distinction between the utterly distinct sattva and Purusha; from samyama on what is for the Self (purusha) comes knowledge of Purusha.

3.36
Sanskrit:    ततः प्रातिभश्रावणवेदनादर्शास्वादवार्ता जायन्ते
IAST:        tataḥ prātibha-śrāvaṇa-vedanādarśāsvāda-vārtā jāyante
English:     From this arise intuition, supernatural hearing, touch, sight, taste, and smell.

3.37
Sanskrit:    ते समाधावुपसर्गा व्युत्थाने सिद्धयः
IAST:        te samādhāv upasargā vyutthāne siddhayaḥ
English:     These are obstacles in samadhi, yet they are powers in the outward-turned state.

3.38
Sanskrit:    बन्धकारणशैथिल्यात्प्रचारसंवेदनाच्च चित्तस्य परशरीरावेशः
IAST:        bandha-kāraṇa-śaithilyāt pracāra-saṃvedanāc ca cittasya para-śarīrāveśaḥ
English:     By loosening the causes of bondage and by knowledge of passage, the mind can enter another body.

3.39
Sanskrit:    उदानजयाज्जलपङ्ककण्टकादिष्वसङ्ग उत्क्रान्तिश्च
IAST:        udāna-jayāj jala-paṅka-kaṇṭakādiṣv asaṅga utkrāntiś ca
English:     From mastery of udana vayu comes non-contact with water, mud, thorns, and so forth, and the power of levitation.

3.40
Sanskrit:    समानजयाज्ज्वलनम्
IAST:        samāna-jayāj jvalanam
English:     From mastery of samana vayu comes radiance.

3.41
Sanskrit:    श्रोत्राकाशयोः सम्बन्धसंयमाद्दिव्यं श्रोत्रम्
IAST:        śrotrākāśayoḥ sambandha-saṃyamād divyaṃ śrotram
English:     From samyama on the relationship between the ear and space comes divine hearing.

3.42
Sanskrit:    कायाकाशयोः सम्बन्धसंयमाल्लघुतूलसमापत्तेश्चाकाशगमनम्
IAST:        kāyākāśayoḥ sambandha-saṃyamāl laghu-tūla-samāpatteś cākāśa-gamanam
English:     From samyama on the relationship between body and space, and absorption on the lightness of cotton, comes the ability to travel through space.

3.43
Sanskrit:    बहिरकल्पिता वृत्तिर्महाविदेहा ततः प्रकाशावरणक्षयः
IAST:        bahir akalpitā vṛttir mahāvidehā tataḥ prakāśāvaraṇa-kṣayaḥ
English:     The external, unimagined fluctuation is the great bodiless state; from that, the veil over the light of knowledge is destroyed.

3.44
Sanskrit:    स्थूलस्वरूपसूक्ष्मान्वयार्थवत्त्वसंयमाद्भूतजयः
IAST:        sthūla-svarūpa-sūkṣmānvayārthavattva-saṃyamād bhūta-jayaḥ
English:     From samyama on the gross, essential, subtle, inherent, and purposive aspects comes mastery of the elements.

3.45
Sanskrit:    ततोऽणिमादिप्रादुर्भावः कायसम्पत्तद्धर्मानभिघातश्च
IAST:        tato 'ṇimādi-prādurbhāvaḥ kāya-sampat tad-dharmānabhighātaś ca
English:     From that arise the manifestation of the eight powers (anima, etc.), perfection of the body, and freedom from the obstructive power of the elements.

3.46
Sanskrit:    रूपलावण्यबलवज्रसंहननत्वानि कायसम्पत्
IAST:        rūpa-lāvaṇya-bala-vajra-saṃhananatvāni kāya-sampat
English:     Beauty, grace, strength, and adamantine firmness constitute perfection of the body.

3.47
Sanskrit:    ग्रहणस्वरूपास्मितान्वयार्थवत्त्वसंयमादिन्द्रियजयः
IAST:        grahaṇa-svarūpāsmitānvayārthavattva-saṃyamād indriya-jayaḥ
English:     From samyama on the power of perception, essential nature, ego, inherence, and purposiveness comes mastery of the senses.

3.48
Sanskrit:    ततो मनोजवित्वं विकरणभावः प्रधानजयश्च
IAST:        tato mano-javitvaṃ vikaraṇa-bhāvaḥ pradhāna-jayaś ca
English:     From that arise swiftness of mind, the state beyond the senses, and mastery of the primary cause (pradhana).

3.49
Sanskrit:    सत्त्वपुरुषान्यताख्यातिमात्रस्य सर्वभावाधिष्ठातृत्वं सर्वज्ञातृत्वं च
IAST:        sattva-puruṣānyatā-khyāti-mātrasya sarva-bhāvādhiṣṭhātṛtvaṃ sarva-jñātṛtvaṃ ca
English:     Only for one who knows the distinction between sattva and Purusha come omnipotence and omniscience.

3.50
Sanskrit:    तद्वैराग्यादपि दोषबीजक्षये कैवल्यम्
IAST:        tad-vairāgyād api doṣa-bīja-kṣaye kaivalyam
English:     By non-attachment even to that, and with the destruction of the seeds of imperfection, comes kaivalya (liberation).

3.51
Sanskrit:    स्थान्युपनिमन्त्रणे सङ्गस्मयाकरणं पुनरनिष्टप्रसङ्गात्
IAST:        sthāny-upanimantraṇe saṅga-smayākaraṇaṃ punar aniṣṭa-prasaṅgāt
English:     Upon invitation from celestial beings, one should not succumb to attachment or pride, lest undesirable consequences arise again.

3.52
Sanskrit:    क्षणतत्क्रमयोः संयमाद्विवेकजं ज्ञानम्
IAST:        kṣaṇa-tat-kramayoḥ saṃyamād vivekajaṃ jñānam
English:     From samyama on the moment and its sequence comes knowledge born of discrimination.

3.53
Sanskrit:    जातिलक्षणदेशैरन्यतानवच्छेदात्तुल्ययोस्ततः प्रतिपत्तिः
IAST:        jāti-lakṣaṇa-deśair anyatānavacchedāt tulyayos tataḥ pratipattiḥ
English:     From this comes the ability to distinguish things that are otherwise indistinguishable by class, characteristic, or position.

3.54
Sanskrit:    तारकं सर्वविषयं सर्वथाविषयमक्रमं चेति विवेकजं ज्ञानम्
IAST:        tārakaṃ sarva-viṣayaṃ sarvathā-viṣayam akramaṃ ceti vivekajaṃ jñānam
English:     The knowledge born of discrimination is the deliverer; it encompasses all objects, in all ways, simultaneously, transcending sequence.

3.55
Sanskrit:    सत्त्वपुरुषयोः शुद्धिसाम्ये कैवल्यमिति
IAST:        sattva-puruṣayoḥ śuddhi-sāmye kaivalyam iti
English:     When sattva and Purusha are equal in purity, kaivalya arises.


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    PADA IV — KAIVALYA PADA (On Liberation)
                           34 Sutras
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

4.1
Sanskrit:    जन्मौषधिमन्त्रतपःसमाधिजाः सिद्धयः
IAST:        janmauṣadhi-mantra-tapaḥ-samādhijāḥ siddhayaḥ
English:     The supernatural powers arise from birth, herbs, mantras, austerity, or samadhi.

4.2
Sanskrit:    जात्यन्तरपरिणामः प्रकृत्यापूरात्
IAST:        jātyantara-pariṇāmaḥ prakṛtyāpūrāt
English:     The transformation into another form of existence arises by the overflow of nature.

4.3
Sanskrit:    निमित्तमप्रयोजकं प्रकृतीनां वरणभेदस्तु ततः क्षेत्रिकवत्
IAST:        nimittam aprayojakaṃ prakṛtīnāṃ varaṇa-bhedas tu tataḥ kṣetrikavat
English:     The incidental cause does not directly cause nature to transform; it only removes obstacles, like a farmer clearing obstructions to let water flow.

4.4
Sanskrit:    निर्माणचित्तान्यस्मितामात्रात्
IAST:        nirmāṇa-cittāny asmitā-mātrāt
English:     Constructed minds arise from ego-sense alone.

4.5
Sanskrit:    प्रवृत्तिभेदे प्रयोजकं चित्तमेकमनेकेषाम्
IAST:        pravṛtti-bhede prayojakaṃ cittam ekam anekeṣām
English:     Though the activities of the many constructed minds differ, the one original mind directs them all.

4.6
Sanskrit:    तत्र ध्यानजमनाशयम्
IAST:        tatra dhyānajam anāśayam
English:     Among these, the mind born of meditation is free from impressions.

4.7
Sanskrit:    कर्माशुक्लाकृष्णं योगिनस्त्रिविधमितरेषाम्
IAST:        karma-aśuklākṛṣṇaṃ yoginas trividham itareṣām
English:     The karma of a yogi is neither white nor black; for others it is of three kinds.

4.8
Sanskrit:    ततस्तद्विपाकानुगुणानामेवाभिव्यक्तिर्वासनानाम्
IAST:        tatas tad-vipākānuguṇānām evābhivyaktir vāsanānām
English:     From these, only those impressions whose ripening fits the condition come to manifestation.

4.9
Sanskrit:    जातिदेशकालव्यवहितानामप्यानन्तर्यं स्मृतिसंस्कारयोरेकरूपत्वात्
IAST:        jāti-deśa-kāla-vyavahitānām apy ānantaryaṃ smṛti-saṃskārayor eka-rūpatvāt
English:     Because memory and impressions are of one form, there is an unbroken sequence even when separated by birth, place, or time.

4.10
Sanskrit:    तासामनादित्वं चाशिषो नित्यत्वात्
IAST:        tāsām anāditvaṃ cāśiṣo nityatvāt
English:     And these impressions are beginningless, since the desire to live is eternal.

4.11
Sanskrit:    हेतुफलाश्रयालम्बनैः संगृहीतत्वादेषामभावे तदभावः
IAST:        hetu-phalāśrayālambanaiḥ saṃgṛhītatvād eṣām abhāve tad-abhāvaḥ
English:     Since these impressions are held together by cause, effect, substrate, and support, with the disappearance of these, they disappear.

4.12
Sanskrit:    अतीतानागतं स्वरूपतोऽस्त्यध्वभेदाद्धर्माणाम्
IAST:        atītānāgataṃ svarūpato 'sty adhva-bhedād dharmāṇām
English:     Past and future exist in their own form; the difference of characteristics is what makes them belong to different times.

4.13
Sanskrit:    ते व्यक्तसूक्ष्मा गुणात्मानः
IAST:        te vyakta-sūkṣmā guṇātmānaḥ
English:     They are manifest or subtle in nature, being composed of the gunas.

4.14
Sanskrit:    परिणामैकत्वाद्वस्तुतत्त्वम्
IAST:        pariṇāmaikatvād vastu-tattvam
English:     The reality of an object is due to the unity of its transformations.

4.15
Sanskrit:    वस्तुसाम्ये चित्तभेदात्तयोर्विभक्तः पन्थाः
IAST:        vastu-sāmye citta-bhedāt tayor vibhaktaḥ panthāḥ
English:     Though the object is the same, the paths of the mind and the object are separate, since the minds are different.

4.16
Sanskrit:    न चैकचित्ततन्त्रं वस्तु तदप्रमाणकं तदा किं स्यात्
IAST:        na caikacittantraṃ vastu tadapramāṇakaṃ tadā kiṃ syāt
English:     Nor is an object dependent on a single mind; if it were, what would happen to it when that mind is absent?

4.17
Sanskrit:    तदुपरागापेक्षित्वाच्चित्तस्य वस्तु ज्ञाताज्ञातम्
IAST:        tad-uparāgāpekṣitvāc cittasya vastu jñātājñātam
English:     An object is known or unknown depending on whether the mind is colored by it.

4.18
Sanskrit:    सदा ज्ञाताश्चित्तवृत्तयस्तत्प्रभोः पुरुषस्यापरिणामित्वात्
IAST:        sadā jñātāś citta-vṛttayas tat-prabhoḥ puruṣasyāpariṇāmitvāt
English:     The fluctuations of the mind are always known to their master, Purusha, who is changeless.

4.19
Sanskrit:    न तत्स्वाभासं दृश्यत्वात्
IAST:        na tat svābhāsaṃ dṛśyatvāt
English:     The mind is not self-luminous, since it is an object of perception.

4.20
Sanskrit:    एकसमये चोभयानवधारणम्
IAST:        eka-samaye cobhayānavadhāraṇam
English:     Nor can it perceive both subject and object simultaneously.

4.21
Sanskrit:    चित्तान्तरदृश्ये बुद्धिबुद्धेरतिप्रसङ्गः स्मृतिसंकरश्च
IAST:        cittāntara-dṛśye buddhi-buddher atiprasaṅgaḥ smṛti-saṃkaraś ca
English:     If the mind were seen by another mind, there would be infinite regression and confusion of memory.

4.22
Sanskrit:    चितेरप्रतिसंक्रमायास्तदाकारापत्तौ स्वबुद्धिसंवेदनम्
IAST:        citer apratisaṃkramāyās tad-ākārāpattau sva-buddhi-saṃvedanam
English:     When the unchanging Consciousness assumes the form of the intellect, it becomes aware of its own intellect.

4.23
Sanskrit:    द्रष्टृदृश्योपरक्तं चित्तं सर्वार्थम्
IAST:        draṣṭṛ-dṛśyoparaktaṃ cittaṃ sarvārtham
English:     The mind, colored by both Seer and seen, comprehends all objects.

4.24
Sanskrit:    तदसंख्येयवासनाभिश्चित्रमपि परार्थं संहत्यकारित्वात्
IAST:        tad asaṃkhyeya-vāsanābhiś citram api parārthaṃ saṃhatya-kāritvāt
English:     Though the mind is variegated with countless impressions, it exists for the sake of another (Purusha), because it acts in conjunction.

4.25
Sanskrit:    विशेषदर्शिन आत्मभावभावनाविनिवृत्तिः
IAST:        viśeṣa-darśina ātma-bhāva-bhāvanā-vinivṛttiḥ
English:     For one who has seen the distinction, the cultivation of self-existence ceases.

4.26
Sanskrit:    तदा विवेकनिम्नं कैवल्यप्राग्भारं चित्तम्
IAST:        tadā viveka-nimnaṃ kaivalya-prāgbhāraṃ cittam
English:     Then the mind, inclined toward discrimination, gravitates toward kaivalya.

4.27
Sanskrit:    तच्छिद्रेषु प्रत्ययान्तराणि संस्कारेभ्यः
IAST:        tac-chidreṣu pratyayāntarāṇi saṃskārebhyaḥ
English:     In the gaps, other cognitions arise from impressions.

4.28
Sanskrit:    हानमेषां क्लेशवदुक्तम्
IAST:        hānam eṣāṃ kleśavad uktam
English:     Their removal is said to be like that of the afflictions.

4.29
Sanskrit:    प्रसंख्यानेऽप्यकुसीदस्य सर्वथा विवेकख्यातेर्धर्ममेघः समाधिः
IAST:        prasaṃkhyāne 'py akusīdasya sarvathā viveka-khyāter dharma-meghaḥ samādhiḥ
English:     For one who has no interest even in highest illumination, and who maintains perpetual discriminative discernment, the samadhi called "dharma megha" (cloud of virtue) arises.

4.30
Sanskrit:    ततः क्लेशकर्मनिवृत्तिः
IAST:        tataḥ kleśa-karma-nivṛttiḥ
English:     From that comes cessation of afflictions and karma.

4.31
Sanskrit:    तदा सर्वावरणमलापेतस्य ज्ञानस्यानन्त्याज्ज्ञेयमल्पम्
IAST:        tadā sarvāvaraṇa-malāpetasya jñānasyānantyāj jñeyam alpam
English:     Then, because knowledge is infinite — with all veils and impurities removed — what remains to be known is but little.

4.32
Sanskrit:    ततः कृतार्थानां परिणामक्रमसमाप्तिर्गुणानाम्
IAST:        tataḥ kṛtārthānāṃ pariṇāma-krama-samāptir guṇānām
English:     Then, with their purpose fulfilled, the sequence of transformation of the gunas comes to an end.

4.33
Sanskrit:    क्षणप्रतियोगी परिणामापरान्तनिर्ग्राह्यः क्रमः
IAST:        kṣaṇa-pratiyogī pariṇāmāparānta-nirgrāhyaḥ kramaḥ
English:     Sequence corresponds to moments; it is apprehensible at the final end of transformation.

4.34
Sanskrit:    पुरुषार्थशून्यानां गुणानां प्रतिप्रसवः कैवल्यं स्वरूपप्रतिष्ठा वा चितिशक्तिरिति
IAST:        puruṣārtha-śūnyānāṃ guṇānāṃ pratiprasavaḥ kaivalyaṃ svarūpa-pratiṣṭhā vā citi-śaktir iti
English:     Kaivalya is the return of the gunas to their source — empty of purpose for Purusha — or it is the establishment of the power of pure Consciousness in its own nature.
